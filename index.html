This engine is doing far more work with far less displacement than its ancestor.
The drop in cylinder count and displacement — combined with forced induction — means every component runs hotter and under higher pressure for longer. On paper, performance numbers are similar or better. In practice, mechanical margin is dramatically reduced. This is the trend the video describes.
How to read this

hp/L (horsepower per liter) is the single best proxy for how hard an engine has to work. Higher numbers mean more output is being squeezed from less displacement — usually via turbocharging, higher boost, and tighter calibration.

Modern engines aren't worse engineering. They're operating with less mechanical margin. Less buffer between what they can do and what they have to do.

A note on the data

Specs are pulled from manufacturer publications and represent the most common U.S.-market trim for each generation. Some models offered multiple engines; the headline mainstream option is shown.
